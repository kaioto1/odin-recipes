Rump Steak Recipe

Description

A tender, flavorful cut of beef, rump steak is ideal for pan-searing or grilling. With a simple seasoning and a hot pan, you can get a perfectly seared crust and a juicy medium-rare center — a steakhouse-style result at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 rump steak (about 250–300 g)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • garlic clove (optional), smashed
  • 1–2 sprigs fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)
  • 1 tbsp butter, for basting (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring to room temperature: Let the steak sit out for about 30 minutes before cooking.
  2. Pat dry: Use paper towels to remove surface moisture — this helps form a crust.
  3. Season: Rub both sides with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Preheat pan: Use a cast-iron or heavy skillet, heat over high until it just starts to smoke.
  5. Sear: Place the steak in the pan. Don’t move it for 2–3 minutes. Flip and cook the other side.
  6. Optional basting: In the last 1–2 minutes, reduce heat, add butter, garlic, and herbs. Spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.
  7. Check doneness:
    • Rare: ~2 mins/side
    • Medium-rare: ~3 mins/side
    • Medium: ~4 mins/side
  8. Rest: Transfer to a plate, tent with foil, and rest for 5–7 minutes before slicing.
